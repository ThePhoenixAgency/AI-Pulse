Apple Stock Today: AAPL Drops Below $260 After Recent High

Source: The Mac Observer | Date: 2/13/2026 | Lang: EN | Original Article

Apple Inc. (NASDAQ: AAPL) shares opened the week under pressure and closed lower on Friday trading. The stock hit a low near $255.45 and was last quoted at $255.78, down about 2.3% on the session. This move came after a previous close at $261.73 and reflected heavier trading, with around 55 million shares changing hands, slightly above average volume.

Investors have seen volatility in Apple’s price over the past weeks. After climbing earlier in February to levels near $278, the stock retraced, driven by profit-taking and broader tech pressure. Recent trading data shows swings within a range from roughly $260 to $280 before Friday’s decline.

What Is Driving the Price Move

Recent headlines have influenced how traders view Apple stock:

  • Apple’s stock dropped sharply on one session, marking one of its worst days this year.
  • Concerns on delays to its AI-driven services, especially the Siri upgrade, have weighed on sentiment.
  • A broader selloff in tech shares has kept pressure on Apple and peers.

Analysts point to a mix of fundamental and sentiment-driven forces. Product strength and revenue remain substantial, but uncertainty about AI integration timelines has eroded some confidence in the share price.

Key Price and Volume Figures

Here are the notable price figures around Apple stock today:

  • Closing price Friday: $255.78
  • Intra-day low: ~$255.45
  • Recent four-week high: Near $278
  • 52-week range: Approx. $169 to $289
  • Trading volume: ~55 million shares (higher than average)

These figures show that while Apple remains well above long-term lows, it has pulled back from recent highs.

Market Context and What to Watch

Tech markets influence Apple’s movement every session. The broader “Magnificent Seven” group, including Apple, has seen volatility tied to debates over growth and AI spending.

Investors will likely watch the following catalysts:

  • Updates on Apple’s AI and Siri rollout plans
  • Quarterly earnings and forward guidance
  • Broader tech sector trends
  • Major product launches or supply chain shifts

These elements can affect how traders and longer-term holders price AAPL in the weeks ahead.
