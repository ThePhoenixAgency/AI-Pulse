Show HN: Finnish Humanizer – 26 patterns for detecting AI-generated Finnish text

Source: Hacker News Show | Date: 2/22/2026 2:10:17 PM | Lien | Lang: EN

Finnish is morphologically complex — vowel harmony, six grammatical cases, free word order. AI models get it wrong in the same predictable ways every time. I built this after noticing that AI-generated Finnish triggers immediate pattern recognition in native speakers: overly formal register, SVO word order (Finnish allows much more variation), missing discourse particles (-han/-hän, -pa/-pä), and excessive nominalization. Finnish Humanizer is a pattern library — 26 identified patterns — packaged as a Claude Code skill and distributed for 15 platforms (Cursor, Copilot, Windsurf, ChatGPT custom instructions, etc.). It's not an additional model call. It's a checklist of linguistic tells you can apply mechanically. Patterns are grounded in Finnish linguistics research (Kotus — the Research Institute for the Languages of Finland).
