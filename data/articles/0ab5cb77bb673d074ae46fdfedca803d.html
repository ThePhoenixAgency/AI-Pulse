Apple TV has a new Valentine’s Day release, and it leans into romance with a strange, funny twist. ‘Eternity’, a romantic comedy film starring Elizabeth Olsen, Miles Teller, and Callum Turner, is now available to stream on Apple TV after its earlier theatrical run.

The movie sets up its hook fast. After death, people arrive at a place where they must choose their “living arrangement” for the rest of forever. That choice turns personal for Joan (Olsen) because she does not arrive alone in this afterlife.

Joan must decide whether to spend eternity with Larry (Teller), her long-term husband, or Luke (Turner), her first love who died young and has been waiting for her for decades.

Plot

The afterlife in Eternity works like a waiting room with consequences. Everyone gets time to decide where they want to go and who they want to be with. That rule sounds clean until Joan faces a choice that reopens a whole life of memories.

Larry’s decision adds pressure. When he dies, he chooses to wait for Joan instead of moving on right away. Then he learns Luke has already been waiting for her for more than 50 years, holding onto the version of Joan he loved before the rest of her life happened.

That sets up the core tension: Joan is not choosing between two dates. She is choosing between two chapters of herself, and the movie keeps returning to that idea as the love triangle turns into a question about time, loyalty, and regret.
