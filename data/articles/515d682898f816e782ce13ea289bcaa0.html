A Simple Way to Catch Technical SEO Issues Early with Webpulls

Source: Dev.to Open Source | Date: 2/14/2026 | Lang: EN | Original Article

Technical SEO is often overlooked during development because it is not as visible as design or features, yet it strongly influences how search engines crawl, index, and rank a website.

A site can look perfect to users while still suffering from broken links, duplicate metadata, redirect chains, or crawlability issues that quietly reduce its search visibility.

For developers who already value clean architecture and performance, paying attention to technical SEO is simply an extension of good development practice. Using a crawler such as Webpulls provides a practical way to monitor this.

It scans websites the way search engine bots do and highlights structural and on-page issues before they become serious ranking problems. Instead of manually checking pages, developers can quickly review status codes, metadata consistency, URL structures, and internal linking patterns, then fix high-impact issues early.

Integrating periodic crawls into deployment or QA workflows helps maintain a technically healthy site over time.

In the long run, consistent technical SEO checks support better discoverability, smoother user experience, and more sustainable website growth.
