France and much of Europe saw heavy snow overnight and into the morning on Wednesday as the French capital saw a rare stretch of sub-zero temperatures. A thick blanket of snowfall glistened on streets and monuments across Paris, with some residents resorting to skiing their way across the city.

Snow and ice are falling on northern and western France in a phenomenon of "rare magnitude", according to the Metéo-France weather agency. More than 100 flights at Charles de Gaulle airport and another 40 at Orly were cancelled on Wednesday, with the French capital halting bus services starting from around 7am.
