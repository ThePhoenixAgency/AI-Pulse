I Curated an Awesome List for SEC Filing Tools & Resources

Source: Dev.to Open Source | Date: 2/17/2026 5:49:07 AM | Lien | Lang: EN

If you've ever tried to figure out what hedge funds or institutional investors are buying and selling, you've probably stumbled across SEC filings. The SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission) requires large investment managers to disclose their holdings quarterly through 13F filings, and companies file 10-Ks, 10-Qs, and 8-Ks for earnings, annual reports, and material events. The problem? There's a ton of tools, APIs, libraries, and datasets scattered across the internet for working with this data — but no single place to find them all. So I made one: awesome-sec-filings I organized everything into categories that made sense to me as a developer: Platforms & Tools — Web apps for browsing and analyzing filings (13F Insight, WhaleWisdom, OpenInsider, etc.) Libraries & SDKs — Python, R, and JavaScript packages for parsing EDGAR data programmatically (edgartools, sec-api, py-edgar) APIs — REST APIs for structured access to filing data Datasets — Pre-processed datasets on Kaggle, HuggingFace, and academic sources Tutorials & Articles — Guides for getting started with SEC data Official Resources — Direct links to EDGAR, XBRL tools, and SEC developer resources I've been working on 13F Insight, a platform for tracking institutional investor holdings. While building it, I kept bookmarking useful tools and resources. Eventually my bookmarks folder got out of hand, so I figured — why not turn it into an awesome list? The finance/investing space has some great awesome lists already (shoutout to awesome-quant!), but none focused specifically on SEC filings.
