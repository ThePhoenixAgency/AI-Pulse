Mastering the ARC Raiders Loot System

The core loop of the Arc Raiders game revolves around three critical decisions: keep, sell, or recycle. Every item you find in ARC Raiders—from a broken alarm clock to a pristine ARC memory core—has a purpose.

  • Keep: Quest items and high-tier crafting components should always be prioritized. Rare ARC parts like the Leaper Pulse Unit or Spotter Relay are essential for Hideout upgrades in ARC Raiders.
  • Sell: Valuables with no utility, such as "Diamond" trinkets or surplus consumables, should be sold to traders for Credits to fund your next Arc Raiders game run.
  • Recycle: Broken electronics and scrap metal are often worth more as raw materials than their sell price. Recycling is crucial for crafting ammo and weapon attachments to survive the dangers of ARC Raiders.

Surviving the Machines: ARC Intel

The ARC machines are relentless. To defeat them in ARC Raiders, you must study their behaviors and weaknesses. Our database provides detailed intel on every machine type found in the Arc Raiders game.

  • Drones: Small, agile units like Ticks and Wasps often swarm raiders. Use fast-firing weapons or AoE explosives to clear these common ARC Raiders threats.
  • Walkers: Heavily armored units like the Devastator require armor-piercing rounds. Target their exposed cooling vents for critical damage to take down these Arc Raiders game giants.
  • Titans: Massive threats that require coordinated squad tactics to bring down. Avoid direct confrontation unless fully equipped with top-tier ARC Raiders gear.

Where to Find Loot: ARC Raiders Map Guide

Knowing where to look is half the battle in the Arc Raiders game. Speranza is divided into several massive regions, each with unique biomes, danger levels, and loot pools. Understanding these zones is key to efficient farming runs in ARC Raiders.

Key Operations Areas

  • Buried CityA dense urban ruin submerged in sand. This map is characterized by close-quarters combat inside skyscrapers and perilous traversals across open dunes. It is the premier location for finding Old World Relics and Residential loot like Dog Collars and Electronics. Verticality is key here; use ziplines to stay above the fray in this classic ARC Raiders environment.
  • Calabreson (Spaceport)Dominated by massive launch structures and open tarmac. The Spaceport offers high-tier Industrial and Military loot. Expect long-range engagements with Rocketeers and Snitches. The hangars are goldmines for weapon parts but are often heavily guarded by Squadrons of machines in this dangerous Arc Raiders game zone.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best way to farm credits in ARC Raiders?

Focus on collecting high-value trinkets (marked with a diamond icon) and completing Trader contracts. Residential zones in the Buried City map are often rich in sellable valuables in the Arc Raiders game.

How do I unlock new weapons?

Most weapons are unlocked by increasing your reputation with Traders. You can also find blueprints in Security Zones which allow you to craft weapons at your Hideout's workshop, a core feature of ARC Raiders.

Is ARC Raiders free to play?

No, ARC Raiders is not free-to-play. Embark Studios shifted the Arc Raiders game from its original free-to-play model to a premium, paid experience, with a planned price point of approximately $40. The decision was made to ensure a better, more respectful experience for players, moving away from microtransaction-heavy models.

Can I play ARC Raiders solo?

While ARC Raiders is designed with squad-play in mind, solo play is fully supported. Solo raiders in the Arc Raiders game must rely on stealth and guerilla tactics to survive against larger groups and tough ARC machines.
